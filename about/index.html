What is Basstatic

Basstatic is a cultural movement centered around bass, movement, and inner state.

It is a space where music becomes a tool for release, expression, and transformation. A place where people come not just to dance, but to reconnect with themselves through vibration, rhythm, and presence.

At the core of Basstatic is the idea that sound — especially low frequencies — affects the body, the mind, and the emotional field. Through deep bass and conscious movement, the dance becomes a form of meditation, a reset, and a way to return to an authentic state.

It is not a party in the usual sense.
It is a shared experience.
A field where individuality and unity exist at the same time.

History

Basstatic began in Goa in January 2020 as an exploration of bass music as a conscious dance practice.

From there, the movement continued to grow on Koh Phangan, where it found a natural home within an open and experimental community. Over time, gatherings became regular, and the dance evolved into a recognizable format and atmosphere that people returned to again and again.

Today, Basstatic continues to live through weekly events on Koh Phangan, carried forward by the local community, while a new chapter is being activated in Bali with regular sessions planned twice a month.

Rather than being tied to one place, Basstatic moves with the people who carry its spirit — adapting, evolving, and reappearing in new locations.

The Idea

Basstatic is about entering a state.

A state where the mind becomes quieter, the body takes over, and emotions find their natural expression through movement.

The practice is rooted in the understanding that dance has always been part of human culture — from ancient ritual movement to modern bass-driven gatherings. It is both personal and collective, tribal and individual at the same time.

There is no performance, no judgment, no expectations.
Only sound, movement, and presence.

Sound

The musical foundation of Basstatic is built around bass-driven genres, including:

  • Dub
  • UK Garage
  • Dubstep
  • Jungle
  • Drum & Bass

All styles are connected by one element — deep, physical low frequencies that can be felt in the body and guide the movement naturally.
